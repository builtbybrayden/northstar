Navigation patterns

Four alternatives to the standard iOS tab bar. Each mockup shows the same "Today" surface so they're directly comparable. Below each, what it costs you and what it buys you.

1 · Floating pill bar

A blurred capsule that hovers above the content. Active tab expands to show its label; others stay as icons.

+ Reads modern. Animated, premium feel. Reuses iOS Liquid Glass.

− Still bottom-anchored — same one-handed reach as default tabs.

2 · Side rail (left edge)

Vertical strip pinned to the left edge. Always-visible icons; tap any to swap pillars. Active gets an accent bar.

+ Frees vertical content. Thumb-friendly on big phones. Scales to iPad with zero work.

− Unconventional on iOS — costs you a moment of "what is this?"

3 · Top segments + swipe

A small segment header up top; swipe horizontally to move between pillars. Dots at the bottom hint at position.

+ Fastest pillar-to-pillar feel. Gesture-native. No persistent chrome at all.

− Discoverability — users may not realize they can swipe between pillars.

Recommendation

For a personal Life-OS, #1 (floating pill bar) is the strongest baseline — it keeps the muscle memory of iOS tabs while looking meaningfully more polished, and it's a 1-day swap from the current TabView. A second pass could layer #4 (FAB + command palette) on top: keep the pill bar for spatial navigation, add a center "✨" FAB for actions ("Ask", "Log dose", "Add milestone") — the two patterns complement rather than compete. #3 (swipe pager) is tempting but discoverability is the price; reserve it for inside-pillar sub-tabs (e.g., Health → Recovery / Sleep / Strain / Stack). #2 (side rail) is the iPad story — worth revisiting when you build that surface.
